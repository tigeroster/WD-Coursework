The 12 Most Memorable Moments in Sports History

The majority of the sports world has been put on hold, and it seems like a lifetime since we last watched a fan-attended major sporting event on TV. As professional leagues make plans for their return, we've gathered some of sports' most important and memorable moments, from history-making legends and dynasties to the triumphs that rallied a nation.

Tiger Woods Wins the 1997 Masters

After becoming the youngest Masters winner at 21, Woods embraced his father, Earl, in an emotional moment that would mark the beginning of his storied career.

The Miracle on Ice

Against the backdrop of the Cold War, the story of the gritty underdog U.S. hockey team and their unlikely defeat of the powerhouse Soviet Union at the 1980 Olympics will live on in the hearts of Americans forever.

Serena Williams Wins First Grand Slam

One of the greatest athletes of all time, Williams captured her first Grand Slam win as a 17-year- old at the 1999 U.S. Open.

President George W. Bush's First Pitch

Wearing an FDNY jacket, 43 took the mound at Yankee Stadium during the 2001 World Series, just seven weeks after the attacks of 9/11. “I was saying to the crowd, ‘I’m with you, the country’s with you,’” the president said.

Michael Jordan's Final Shot as a Chicago Bull

Jordan clinched the 1998 NBA title with a game-winning shot from the top of the key—his last in a Bulls uniform.

Brandi Chastain's World Cup-Winning PK

Winning Team USA the FIFA Women’s World Cup in front of 90,000 fans.

Jackie Robinson Breaks the Color Barrier

Robinson became the first African American to play in Major League Baseball, starting at first base for the Brooklyn Dodgers and forever changing the complexion and history of the game.

Rumble in the Jungle

The history-making boxing event in Kinshasa, Zaire, featured a dethroned Muhammad Ali knocking out heavyweight champ George Foreman in eight rounds to take back his crown.

Mary Lou Retton's Perfect 10

Before she was a Wheaties box sensation, 16-year-old Retton earned two perfect 10s on floor and vault at the 1984 Olympics

Broken Curses

In 2005, the Chicago White Sox broke the 85-year Curse of the Black Sox (a gambling scandal) with a World Series win.

Miami Dolphins Remain Perfect

Miami Dolphins become the first—and only, to this day— NFL team to go undefeated throughout the regular season and playoffs

NC State's Cinderella Story

Beating Houston’s future NBA Hall of Famers Clyde Drexler and Hakeem Olajuwon in the 1983 NCAA National Championship.
